Squat

  • How to do:

    1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart.

    2. Lower your hips back and down as if sitting in a chair.

    3. Keep your chest up and knees behind your toes.

    4. Return to standing.

  • Target Muscles:

    Muscles in the thighs, buttocks, lower back, and core.

  • Benefits:

    Builds strength in the lower body, improves mobility in the hips and knees, and supports calorie burning through full-body engagement.

Push-Ups

  • How to do:

    1. Begin in a high plank position with your hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.

    2. Maintain a straight line from head to heels while engaging your core.

    3. Lower your body until your chest is just above the floor.

    4. Push through your palms to return to the starting position.

  • Target Muscles:

    Muscles in the chest, shoulders, the back of the upper arms, and the core area.

  • Benefits:

    Improves upper body strength, enhances core stability, and supports functional movement without requiring equipment.

Plank

  • How to do:

    1. Position yourself face down and support your body on your forearms and toes.

    2. Keep your elbows directly beneath your shoulders.

    3. Maintain a straight line from head to heels.

    4. Engage your core and hold the position for the desired duration.

  • Target Muscles:

    Muscles in the abdomen, shoulders, and buttocks.

  • Benefits:

    Improves core strength and stability, supports good posture, and increases endurance in the midsection and shoulders.

Bicep Curl

  • How to do:

    1. Stand upright while holding a dumbbell in each hand with palms facing forward.

    2. Keep your elbows close to your torso.

    3. Curl the weights upward towards your shoulders.

    4. Lower the weights slowly to the starting position.

  • Target Muscles:

    Muscles at the front of the upper arms.

  • Benefits:

    Builds arm strength, tones the upper arms, and improves grip and forearm stability.

Lunges

  • How to do:

    1. Stand upright with feet hip-width apart.

    2. Step one foot forward and lower your hips until both knees form a 90-degree angle.

    3. Ensure the front knee is aligned above the ankle.

    4. Push off the front foot to return to the starting position.

    5. Repeat on the opposite leg.

  • Target Muscles:

    Muscles in the front and back of the thighs, buttocks, and calves.

  • Benefits:

    Strengthens the legs, improves balance and coordination, and increases flexibility in the hip area.
